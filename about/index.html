About Bryan

Staff software engineer focused on distributed systems, performance, and healthy engineering cultures.

Hi, I’m Bryan.

I’m a Staff Software Engineer with a passion for building distributed systems, optimizing performance, and fostering healthy engineering cultures. I hold a Masters in Software Engineering from Johns Hopkins University and have spent over 15 years tackling complex technical challenges.

What I Do

I specialize in designing and developing distributed processing systems. My technical toolkit ranges from low-level C++ and Java to modern TypeScript and Python. Over the years, I’ve focused on:

  • Distributed Systems: Building scalable, high-performance backends and encrypted search algorithms.
  • Cloud and DevOps: Designing AWS-backed services and automating deployments with Terraform, Docker, and CI/CD pipelines.
  • Real-time Data: Implementing near real-time ingestion using PostgreSQL, JMS, and WebSockets.
  • Frontend and Mobile: Shipping responsive SPAs with Vue.js and managing native mobile data protection.

Engineering Philosophy

I believe that great software is a result of both technical rigor and a supportive team environment. I’m a strong advocate for:

  • Mentorship and Leadership: Coaching engineers, developing roadmaps, and establishing norms for psychological safety and cross-team collaboration.
  • Engineering Excellence: Prioritizing design documentation, rigorous code reviews, and building tools that enhance team productivity.
  • Pragmatic Optimization: Whether it’s unrolling a critical loop for performance or refactoring a service for clarity, I focus on the why behind every technical decision.

Why “Empty Catch”?

The name of this site, Empty Catch, is a nod to those moments in programming where we handle an exception but are still fishing for the best solution. It represents the curiosity, trial-and-error, and constant learning that defines the craft of software engineering.

Feel free to reach out if you want to chat about distributed systems, software architecture, or the nuances of modern engineering practices.
