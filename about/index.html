About Bryan

Staff software engineer focused on distributed systems, performance, and healthy engineering cultures.

Hi, I’m Bryan.

I’m a Staff Software Engineer. I’ve been building software for over 15 years, mostly in the distributed systems and real-time data space.

Most of my career has been at startups. My current work is in encrypted search — taking homomorphic encryption research and turning it into something that actually runs in production. That means a lot of C++ performance work, distributed systems design, and close collaboration with cryptography researchers who aren’t always thinking about the engineering constraints. I also lead the core product engineering team, so there’s a management layer on top of the IC work.

Before that I built real-time situational awareness tooling — distributed backend, full-stack SPAs, mobile clients, cloud deployments. Earlier in my career I did defense work: radar simulations, analyst tools, multi-intelligence data fusion. Good place to learn that domain correctness matters as much as clean code.

On the stack side I’m mostly in Java, C++, and TypeScript, with Python and SQL in the mix. Spring Boot, gRPC, PostgreSQL, Spark. Infrastructure with Terraform and Docker on AWS and GCP.

The other part of the job I care about is the team side — design docs, real code reviews, coaching engineers who are figuring out their direction.

Why “Empty Catch”?

Empty catch happens to everyone. You forget to log, a critical error slips to production, and nobody knows why until it’s already a problem. It’s not a sign of a bad engineer — it’s just part of the job. Being able to acknowledge that, own it, and move on without pointing fingers is what actually keeps a team functional. The name is a reminder of that.
